Fax endpoint readiness checklist

Before a fax number is published or handed to a customer, it helps to confirm that the entire receive path is working. A fax endpoint is more than a phone number; it includes routing, document handling, notifications, and internal ownership.

Number and routing

  • The number is assigned to the correct service or device.
  • Inbound routing sends documents to the expected inbox, queue, or user.
  • The receiving team knows who owns failures and retry decisions.

Transmission behavior

  • The endpoint answers consistently during business hours or expected operating hours.
  • Busy or timeout behavior is understood before production use.
  • At least one test page has been received clearly from an outside sender.

Operational follow-through

  • Staff can find the received document without relying on a single person.
  • Retention and privacy rules are understood for any real documents.
  • There is a way to report misrouted or unwanted fax traffic.
